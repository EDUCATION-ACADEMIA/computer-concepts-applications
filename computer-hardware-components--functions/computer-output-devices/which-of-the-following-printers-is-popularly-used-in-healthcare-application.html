Which of the following printers is popularly used in healthcare application?

(A) Laser printer

(B) Inkjet printer

(C) Dot Matrix printer

(D) Wide Format printer

(E) ** 3D printer

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The three most commonly used 3D printer technologies in medical applications are: selective laser sintering (SLS), thermal inkjet (TIJ) printing, and fused deposition modeling (FDM).

Concept note-2: -Fabrication of customized medical devices-including forceps, clamps, hemostats and retractors-are among the most common medical uses for 3D printing. A key benefit is that precise design modifications can be made rapidly based on surgeon feedback.

Concept note-3: -3D printing in medicine can be used to print organ models. These could also be helpful for patient education and pre-operative planning for surgeons. Just recently, scientists are using a combination of MRI and ultrasound imaging along with 3D-printing technology to help doctors prepare for fetal surgeries.

Concept note-4: -Medical device companies like Coalesce use 3D printing to create accurate prototypes of medical devices. 3D printing can accelerate the design process by iterating complex designs in days instead of weeks.
