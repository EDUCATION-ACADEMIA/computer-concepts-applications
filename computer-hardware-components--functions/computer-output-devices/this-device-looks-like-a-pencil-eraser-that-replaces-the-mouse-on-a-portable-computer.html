This device looks like a pencil eraser that replaces the mouse on a portable computer.

(A) ** trackpoint

(B) trackpad

(C) trackball

(D) pointing stick

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A TrackPoint provides users an additional means of interacting with their laptop. The TrackPoint is a small cursor control device located in the center of the keyboard. It is red and textured, and roughly resembles a pencil eraser in appearance. Users can interact with the screen by applying pressure on the TrackPoint.

Concept note-2: -A TrackPoint, also called a pointing stick, is a cursor control device found in IBM ThinkPad notebook computer s. The device is sometimes called an eraser pointer because it is roughly the size and shape of a pencil eraser.

Concept note-3: -In addition to its use in the remote control for a set top computing device and the keyboard of a computer, the TrackPoint device of the present invention is useful in a wide range of input devices.

Concept note-4: -The TrackPoint provides infinite movement without the need to reposition your hand, which is not possible using a mouse. And while trackpads have gotten much larger in recent years, you still have to reposition your finger every so often if you donâ€™t want it to run off the edge.
