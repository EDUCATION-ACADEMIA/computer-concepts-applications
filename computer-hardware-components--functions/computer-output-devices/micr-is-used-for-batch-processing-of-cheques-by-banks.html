MICR is used for batch processing of cheques by banks.

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Magnetic Ink Character Recognition (MICR) Line Magnetic ink character recognition (MICR) is a technology used primarily to identify and process checks. The MICR on a check is the string of characters that appears at the bottom left of the check.

Concept note-2: -“Magnetic Ink Character Recognition” (MICR) is a technology that uses a 9-digit code printed at the bottom of a cheque to quickly and accurately identify and process it. In addition to speeding up the processing of cheques, MICR has other features such as: MICR is located at the bottom of the cheque.

Concept note-3: -MICR (magnetic ink character recognition) is a technology invented in the 1950s that’s used to verify the legitimacy or originality of checks and other paper documents. Special ink, which is sensitive to magnetic fields, is used to print certain characters on the original documents.

Concept note-4: -Magnetic Ink Character Recognition is a character-recognition technology adopted mainly by the banking industry to facilitate the processing of cheques.
