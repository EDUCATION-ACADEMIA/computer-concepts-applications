DAC is an

(A) ** Output devices

(B) Input device

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Stands for “Digital-to-Analog Converter” and is often pronounced “dac.” Since computers only recognize digital information, the output produced by computers is typically in digital format. However, some output devices only accept analog input, which means a digital-to-analog converter, or DAC, must be used.

Concept note-2: -A digital-to-analog converter (DAC) is a circuit that converts a binary input number into an analog output.

Concept note-3: -A Digital to Analog Converter (DAC) converts a digital input signal into an analog output signal. The digital signal is represented with a binary code, which is a combination of bits 0 and 1. This chapter deals with Digital to Analog Converters in detail.

Concept note-4: -A DAC simply converts a digital audio signal into an analog one so that your headphones can then create sound. Much like headphone amplifiers, standalone DACs came about as a response to poor audio quality at the consumer level.

Concept note-5: -A DAC, or digital to analogue converter, is the device that translates digitally stored information from a laptop, iPod or other such device into the analogue sound that we can hear. Why does a signal need to be converted at all?
