Printer that works by spraying ink through nozzles at a sheet of paper.

(A) Output

(B) ** Inkjet Printer

(C) Hard Copy

(D) Projectors

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An inkjet printer is a computer peripheral that produces hard copies of a text document or photo by spraying droplets of ink onto paper. A typical inkjet printer can produce color printing copies with a resolution of 1200 x 1440 dpi.

Concept note-2: -When referring to a non-impact printer, like an inkjet printer, a nozzle is a small hole found on a printer cartridgeâ€™s print head. Nozzles can be smaller than a human hair. Each nozzle produces millions of ink droplets used in creating the images and text generated by the printer.

Concept note-3: -In contrast to laser printing technology, inkjet printers use pigmented liquid ink which is directed into the print head, this then sprays droplets of ink through tiny nozzles onto the page. These microscopic droplets are precisely arranged to recreate images or text.

Concept note-4: -Inkjet printers have dozens of printhead nozzles that move side to side over the paper as it is fed through a roller, spraying microscopic ink droplets onto paper or other media to produce images and text.

Concept note-5: -Most inkjet printers use electroconductive plates to push the ink onto the paper.
