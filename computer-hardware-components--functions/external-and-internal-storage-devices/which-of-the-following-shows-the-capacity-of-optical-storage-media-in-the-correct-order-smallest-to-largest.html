Which of the following shows the capacity of OPTICAL STORAGE MEDIA in the correct order (smallest to largest)?

(A) DVD > BLU RAY > CD

(B) ** CD > DVD > BLU RAY

(C) BLU RAY > DVD > CD

(D) DVD > CD > BLU RAY

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -CDs can store up to 700 MB of data, DVD-DL can store up to 8.5 GB, and Blu-Ray can store between 25 and 128 GB of data.

Concept note-2: -About Optical Storage Device: CD-ROMs are best used for data archiving due to their lower storage capacity.

Concept note-3: -Correct answer is (D) Floppy Disk, Zip Disk, CD-ROM.

Concept note-4: -Option (D) Among the optical storage devices, the blu-ray disk has the highest memory capacity. As the one layered and one-sided blu-ray disc can possess the data up to 50 GB and the double-sided blu-ray hold just double data.
