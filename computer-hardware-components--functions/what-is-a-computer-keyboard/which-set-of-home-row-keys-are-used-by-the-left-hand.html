WHAT IS A COMPUTER KEYBOARD

[SOURCES]
Which set of home row keys are used by the left hand?

(A) ** asdf

(B) mluy

(C) opi,

(D) nm, .

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -On the QWERTY keyboard, the home row keys for the left hand are A, S, D, and F; and J, K, L, and semicolon (; ) for the right hand. The index finger rests on the home key F for the left, and J for the right hand.

Concept note-2: -Your left-hand fingers should be placed over the A, S, D, and F keys, and the right-hand fingers should be placed over the J, K, L, and; keys.

Concept note-3: -On an English keyboard, the home row keys are A-S-D-F and J-K-L-; . The little finger of the left hand sits on the A, the ring finger on the S, the middle finger on the D and the pointer on the F.

Concept note-4: -On the most common type of English language keyboard, the QWERTY layout, “ A S D F “ and “ J K L; “ are the home keys on the home row.

Concept note-5: -On a standard QWERTY keyboard for English speakers the home row keys are: “ASDF” for the left hand and “JKL; ” for the right hand.
