WHAT IS A COMPUTER KEYBOARD

[SOURCES]
Can you guess how many keys are on a Standard keyboard?

(A) Less than 80

(B) ** More than 100

(C) Less than 50

(D) More than 200

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There’s no single standard dictating the number of keys, buttons, or characters on a keyboard; most companies use the PC keyboard with a total of 104 alphanumeric keys as a de facto standard.

Concept note-2: -Answer: There is no specific number as to how many keys a keyboard has. In many computer keyboards, there are 101 keys, some have 102 and some also have 104 keys. There are 6 rows of keys on the keyboard. There are Function keys which are F1-F12.

Concept note-3: -Most keyboards come with 66, 72, or 88 keys. For a beginner, 66 keys are sufficient for learning to play, and you can play most music on a 72-key instrument.

Concept note-4: -A 101-key keyboard (U.S. layout) from IBM that superseded the PC/XT and AT keyboards. Introduced in 1985 as the Model M and standard on IBM’s PS/2 computer, the Enhanced keyboard was used for more than a decade and continues to be used in its Windows keyboard variant.
