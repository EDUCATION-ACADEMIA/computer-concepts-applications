WHAT IS A COMPUTER KEYBOARD

[SOURCES]
A hand-held pointing device that detects two-dimensional motion relative to a surface.

(A) Keyboard

(B) ** Mouse

(C) Monitor

(D) microphone

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A computer mouse (plural mice, also mouses) is a hand-held pointing device that detects two-dimensional motion relative to a surface. This motion is typically translated into the motion of a pointer on a display, which allows a smooth control of the graphical user interface of a computer.

Concept note-2: -Soap mouse – a handheld, position-based pointing device based on existing wireless optical mouse technology. Laser pen – can be used in presentations as a pointing device.

Concept note-3: -Why is a mouse called a pointing device? A mouse is called a pointing device because using it lets you move the mouse cursor and point to anything on the screen.

Concept note-4: -Mouse and joystick are the two common pointing devices.

Concept note-5: -Using a mouse requires a certain type of manual dexterity, therefore, alternative pointing devices are important for inclusion. Alternatives include trackballs, joysticks, trackpads, switches, and transmitter or reflective dots.
