WHAT IS A COMPUTER KEYBOARD

[SOURCES]
Used to strike the letter “a” on the computer keyboard:

(A) right pointer/index finger

(B) ** left pinky

(C) right pinky

(D) none of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Section A keys are pressed by the left little finger. The keys include the back quote, Tab, Caps Locks, Shift, Control, the number 1, letters Q, A, and Z, and Alt. Section B keys are pressed by the left ring finger.

Concept note-2: -The left hand pinky finger starts things off on the “A” key, the ring finger sits on the “S” key, the middle finger takes the “D” and the index the “F.”

Concept note-3: -It is important to position your hands on the keyboard correctly. Your fingers must be in the middle row of your keyboard ont he “HOME ROW” keys. Starting with your left hand, place your little finger (pinky) on key A, ring finger on S, middle finger on D and pointer finger (index finger) on F.

Concept note-4: -If you want to type a capital letter, if the letter you wish to capitalize is being typed by the left hand, use the right-hand pinky to hold down the Shift key; otherwise, use the left-hand pinky to hold down the other Shift key.

Concept note-5: -For example, on the standard QWERTY United States keyboard, the home row keys for your left hand are A, S, D, and F and your right hand are J, K, l, and; (semicolon).
