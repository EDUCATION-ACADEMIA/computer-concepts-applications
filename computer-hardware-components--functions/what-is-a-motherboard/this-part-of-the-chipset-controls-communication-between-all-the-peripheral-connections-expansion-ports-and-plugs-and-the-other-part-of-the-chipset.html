WHAT IS A MOTHERBOARD

[SOURCES]
This part of the chipset controls communication between all the peripheral connections, expansion ports, and plugs, and the other part of the chipset.

(A) CMOS

(B) BIOS

(C) ** Southbridge

(D) Northbridge

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The southbridge chipset controls the rest of the components connected to the computer, including communication between the processor and expansion ports such as USB ports and sound cards.

Concept note-2: -A southbridge chipset handled many of a computer’s I/O functions, such as USB, audio, the system BIOS, the ISA bus or the LPC bus, the low speed PCI/PCIe bus, the IOAPIC interrupt controller, the SATA storage, the historical PATA storage, and the NVMe storage.

Concept note-3: -For PC motherboards, there are two main chipsets: the northbridge and the southbridge. The northbridge is directly connected to the CPU, allowing for faster transmissions between it and ports that require faster speeds. These include your RAM modules and PCI express cards, as well as AGP.

Concept note-4: -Starting with Intel’s 8xx chipsets, Northbridge and Southbridge were changed to Memory Controller and I/O Controller (see Intel Hub Architecture). Northbridge/Southbridge Chipset The Northbridge part of the chipset controls the high-speed channels, while the Southbridge controls the lower speed devices.

Concept note-5: -The north bridge is a controller which controls the flow of data between the CPU and RAM, and to the AGP port.
