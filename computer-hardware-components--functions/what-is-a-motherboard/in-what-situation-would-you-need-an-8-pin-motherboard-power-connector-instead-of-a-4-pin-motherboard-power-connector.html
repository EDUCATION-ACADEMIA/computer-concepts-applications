WHAT IS A MOTHERBOARD

In what situation would you need an 8-pin motherboard power connector instead of a 4-pin motherboard power connector?

(A) ** When providing power to multiple central processing units (CPUs) in the system

(B) When providing more than 12 volts of power to the motherboard

(C) When requiring 192 watts of power to be delivered to the motherboard

(D) When providing power to multiple PCIe slots on the motherboard

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Yours require 65W and peaks up to 90W. I say it should be fine. In short your mobo need 8 pins connector from your PSU to your mobo and 4 pins will never work.

Concept note-2: -A 4-pin connector has a given resistance and running a certain number of amps through the resistance creates a certain voltage drop and generates heat from that resistance. An 8-pin connector will have half the resistance of the 4-pin and thus half the voltage drop and half the heat spread over twice the connector.

Concept note-3: -The 4 pin is only necessary if you are doing extreme overclocking, such as LN2. Otherwise the 8 pin will provide all the power you will need.

Concept note-4: -You do not need to use all the connectors. If you have a low power CPU, you can use a 4-pin power plug on a mobo with an 8-pin socket. But if you have a i9â€“12900K or 5950X you should use as many power connectors as are on the motherboard.
