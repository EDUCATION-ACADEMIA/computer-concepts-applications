WHAT IS A MOTHERBOARD

[SOURCES]
When one port fails, the entire motherboard must be replaced.

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -There are two big reasons for replacing the motherboard. The most obvious reason is that the motherboard is broken. One or more components on the board already burnt-out or lost its function. Sometimes replacing a component is more difficult than replacing one whole board.

Concept note-2: -The motherboard is the main body of a computer. Over time, computer components degrade and may need to be replaced. Replacing the motherboard can help make a computer run more quickly, smoothly and without errors.

Concept note-3: -The motherboard is the computer, so the usual symptom of a failed motherboard is a completely dead system. Fans, drives, and other peripherals may spin up if the motherboard is dead, but more often nothing at all happens when you turn on the power. No beeps, no lights, no fans, nothing.
