WHAT IS A MOTHERBOARD

[SOURCES]
Which of the following motherboard types will fit in a set-top or compact/small form factor chassis?

(A) BTX

(B) AT

(C) ** Mini-ITX

(D) ATX

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Mini-ITX boards can therefore often be used in cases designed for ATX, micro-ATX and other ATX variants if desired.

Concept note-2: -3. The Smallest: Mini-ITX. There’s an even smaller motherboard than the Micro-ATX form factor. The Mini-ITX boards are quite uncommon and measure 6.7 × 6.7 inches.

Concept note-3: -The Mini-ITX form factor uses the same mounting locations and back panel specifications as the ATX form factor, allowing Mini-ITX motherboards to fit in ATX cases.

Concept note-4: -Mini-ITX is a compact motherboard configuration designed to support relatively low-cost computers in small spaces such as in automobiles, set-top box es, and network devices. The Mini-ITX can also be used in making thin client computers.
