WHAT IS A MOTHERBOARD

[SOURCES]
A Nano-ITX motherboard is:

(A) Larger than a Mini-ITX

(B) Larger than a Micro-ATX

(C) ** Smaller than a Mini-ITX

(D) Smaller than Pico-ITX

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Nano-ITX motherboard form factor measures 7.2 cm x 10 cm (2.8” x 3.9") and is 75% smaller than the Mini-ITX form factor.

Concept note-2: -The Pico-ITX is the smallest type of motherboard form factor in this list. Its measurements are 3.9 × 2.8 in and it is 75% smaller than the Mini-ITX. This motherboard was designed and developed by VIA, to open up innovation for smaller and smarter IoT devices.

Concept note-3: -Micro-ATX vs Mini-ITX vs ATX: A Size Comparison Mini-ITX motherboards, on the other hand, are shorter in both height and width than micro-ATX motherboards. They typically only feature a single PCIe lane.

Concept note-4: -Nano-ITX motherboards were another form factor introduced by VIA Technologies in 2003, measuring 120 x 120 mm (4.72 x 4.72 in).

Concept note-5: -A small motherboard form factor can fit into a space constraint and small form-factor industrial computers. Small form factor (SFF) motherboards come in various sizes and shapes: Mini-ITX, 3.5” SBC, 2.5” PICO-ITX, and the smallest 1.8” FEMTO ITX is starting to make an appearance in the embedded market.
