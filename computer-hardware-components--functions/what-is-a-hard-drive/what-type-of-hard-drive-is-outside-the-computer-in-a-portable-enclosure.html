WHAT IS A HARD DRIVE

[SOURCES]
What type of Hard Drive is outside the computer in a portable enclosure?

(A) ** External Hard Drive

(B) Hard Disk Drive

(C) Solid State Drive

(D) Internal Hard Drive

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -An external drive is just a hard drive (HDD) or solid-state drive (SSD) connected to a computer on the outside rather than on the inside.

Concept note-2: -An external hard drive is a portable storage device that can be attached to a computer through a USB or FireWire connection, or wirelessly. External hard drives typically have high storage capacities and are often used to back up computers or serve as a network drive.

Concept note-3: -An external hard drive or SSD is simply a device which is plugged into your machine to give almost-immediate additional storage space, without the need to open your computers internal storage and make upgrades.

Concept note-4: -Most HDDs are found internally in a computer and work as stated above. However, individuals can also purchase external hard drives. External hard drives can be used to expand the storage capacity of a computer or to act as a portable device to back up data.

Concept note-5: -External hard drives, explained The difference between an external hard drive and the hard drive in your computer is that an external drive is portable-you can disconnect it at any time and move it around, without losing the data inside.
