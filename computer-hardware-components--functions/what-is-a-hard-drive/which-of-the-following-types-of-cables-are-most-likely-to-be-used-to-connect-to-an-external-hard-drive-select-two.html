Which of the following types of cables are MOST likely to be used to connect to an external hard drive? (Select TWO).

(A) Molex

(B) SPDIF

(C) ** eSATA

(D) HDMI

(E) ** USB 3.0

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -USB is the most common connection type for external devices, whether external hard drive, thumb drives, flash drives, printers, cameras, all kinds of other devices.

Concept note-2: -SATA Cables for HDDs In a PC setup, SATA cables can be used to connect the hard disk to the motherboard. Hard disks typically feature multiple inlet ports, at least one of which will be SATA compatible.

Concept note-3: -The Micro-B USB 3.0 cable, or simply known as the Micro-B USB cable, is the current standard for external HDD USB cables. Used by most newer versions of external HDDs, it is designed to increase data transfer speed, and only comes in USB 3.0 flavor.

Concept note-4: -Extended SATA (eSATA) cables are used to connect external hard drives or optical drives. They provide transfer speeds that are approximately three times faster than FireWire 400 and USB 2.0.
