How Many GBs does a Terabyte have?

(A) ** 1024

(B) 8

(C) 100

(D) 1

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A terabyte is equal to 1, 024 gigabytes (GB), which itself is equal to 1, 024 megabytes (MB), while a megabyte is equivalent to 1, 024 kilobytes.

Concept note-2: -1Terabyte (TB) is 1000 Gigabytes (GB). 1Tebibyte (TiB) is 1024 Gibibytes (GiB). Tera is a decimal system (base 10) and Tebi is a binary system. Drives are quoted in Decimal, but hardware uses binary, hence why 1000GB drives show as less than that, as they are actually being read and shown in GiB.

Concept note-3: -What is a terabyte? When talking about data storage, we often measure whole system storage capacity in terabytes, but most individual files take up megabytes or gigabytes for large files. So how many gigabytes or megabytes are in a terabyte? 1 TB equals 1, 000 gigabytes (GB) or 1, 000, 000 megabytes (MB).

Concept note-4: -1 Terabyte is equal to 1000 gigabytes (decimal). 1 TB = 103 GB in base 10 (SI).

Concept note-5: -Put simply, 1 terabyte (TB) equals 1, 000 gigabytes (GB) or 1, 000, 000 megabytes (MB). In the case of 2TB, 2 terabytes (TB) equals 2, 000 gigabytes (GB) or 2, 000, 000 megabytes (MB).
