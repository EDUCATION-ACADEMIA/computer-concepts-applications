USING MICROSOFT EXCEL

HOW TO ADD COPY AND MOVE WORKSHEETS WITHIN EXCEL WORKBOOKS

[SOURCES]
Logan wants to insert check boxes and command buttons in her worksheet to limit choices and make it easier for her employees to use. Check boxes and command buttons are types of:

(A) cell styles

(B) ** form controls

(C) macros

(D) themes

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In order to make a check box in Excel, you need to go to Developer Tab—> Controls—> Insert—> Form Controls—> Check Box. Now, click on the cell where you want to place the checkbox; you can position the checkbox by dragging it.

Concept note-2: -On the Insert tab, click Header & Footer. Excel automatically changes to Page Layout view. On Header & Footer tab, click either the Header or Footer, and then select the page number format you want. Header page numbers appear at the top of the printed page, and footer page numbers appear at the bottom.

Concept note-3: -Located just above the Ribbon, the Quick Access Toolbar lets you access common commands no matter which tab is selected. By default, it includes the Save, Undo, and Repeat commands.

Concept note-4: -To insert a new worksheet in front of an existing worksheet, select that worksheet and then, on the Home tab, in the Cells group, click Insert, and then click Insert Sheet. Tip: You can also right-click the tab of an existing worksheet, and then click Insert. On the General tab, click Worksheet, and then click OK.
