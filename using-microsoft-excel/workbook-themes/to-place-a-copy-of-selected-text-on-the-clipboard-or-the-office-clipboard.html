To place a copy of selected text on the Clipboard or the Office Clipboard.

(A) Paste

(B) ** Copy

(C) Cut

(D) Formula

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Select the text or graphics you want to copy, and press Ctrl+C. Each selection appears in the Clipboard, with the latest at the top.

Concept note-2: -For Windows PCs, the Windows clipboard makes it possible to copy one content at a time. However, the limitation is that the old content gets deleted after copying a new one. The Windows clipboard can only retain one copied item at a time. The Office clipboard saves multiple copied items (up to 24) at once.

Concept note-3: -Select the text you want to copy and press Ctrl+C. Place your cursor where you want to paste the copied text and press Ctrl+V.

Concept note-4: -By default, the Clipboard pane is anchored to the left side of the Office program window. If you click the Paste button on the Home tab or press Ctrl+V, you paste the contents of the Windows clipboard, not the Office clipboard. To paste an item from Officeâ€™s clipboard, simply click on that item on the Clipboard pane.
