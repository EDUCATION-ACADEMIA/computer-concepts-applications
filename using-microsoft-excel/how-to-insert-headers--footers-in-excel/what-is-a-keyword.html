What is a keyword?

(A) ** a single word or phrase on your topic

(B) a sentence about your topic

(C) a paragraph about your topic

(D) a question about your topic

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As noted above, a keyword is a word or phrase on your website that matches the words and phrases people are entering into search engines. By learning what keywords or phrases your target audience uses when they search for your product or service, you can create and optimize content to answer their query.

Concept note-2: -Strictly speaking, a keyword means one single word. When the first search engines were launched, they had relatively simple algorithms and many users used single words for their searches. So a key phrase means a set of separate words that build a phrase (so it’s a multi-word search term).

Concept note-3: -Keywords, also commonly called search terms, are the words that you enter into the database search boxes. They represent the main concepts of your research topic and are the words used in everyday life to describe the topic.

Concept note-4: -In Microsoft Word lingo, “keyword tags” or just “tags” usually refers to one or more words that are placed in a document property on the File > Info page. These words can be used in the File Explorer’s search box to find all documents that contain specific tags.

Concept note-5: -Keywords are the words and phrases that people type into search engines to find what they’re looking for. For example, if you were looking to buy a new jacket, you might type something like “mens leather jacket” into Google. Even though that phrase consists of more than one word, it’s still a keyword.
