In a PMT function, the argument indicated by fv refers to ____

(A) ** Future value

(B) Past value

(C) Favorite value

(D) Fixed value

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Fv (optional argument) â€“ This is the future value or a cash balance we want to attain after the last payment is made. If Fv is omitted, it is assumed to be 0 (zero), that is, the future value of a loan is 0.

Concept note-2: -FV, one of the financial functions, calculates the future value of an investment based on a constant interest rate. You can use FV with either periodic, constant payments, or a single lump sum payment. Use the Excel Formula Coach to find the future value of a series of payments.

Concept note-3: -In the PMT function, to what does the Nper argument refer? the number of payments that will be made.

Concept note-4: -What Is Future Value? Future value (FV) is a financial concept that assigns a value to an asset based on estimated variables such as future interest rates or cashflows. It may be useful for an investor to know how much their investment may be in five years given an expected rate of return.

Concept note-5: -The PMT function has two arguments: rate and payment.
