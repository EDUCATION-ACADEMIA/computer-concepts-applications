What are the three types of Sparklines available?

(A) ** Line, Column, Win-Loss

(B) Width, Length, Line

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Sparkline control supports three different sparkline types, namely Line, Column and Winloss, for visualizing data in different context. For example, Line charts are suitable to visualize continuous data, while Column sparklines are used in scenarios where data comparison is involved.

Concept note-2: -A Win-loss Sparkline only shows whether each value is positive or negative, whereas a Sparkline shows how high or low each value is.

Concept note-3: -There are three different types of sparklines: Line, Column, and Win/Loss. Line and Column work the same as line and column charts. Win/Loss is similar to Column, except it only shows whether each value is positive or negative instead of how high or low the values are.

Concept note-4: -A sparkline is a tiny chart in a worksheet cell that provides a visual representation of data. Use sparklines to show trends in a series of values, such as seasonal increases or decreases, economic cycles, or to highlight maximum and minimum values. Position a sparkline near its data for greatest impact.
