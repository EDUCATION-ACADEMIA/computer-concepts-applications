HOW TO MODIFY THE WORKSHEET TAB IN EXCEL

[SOURCES]
What is the name of the feature that saves copies of your files at specified intervals?

(A) Protect Sheet

(B) Track Changes

(C) ** AutoRecover

(D) Document Recovery

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Auto-Recover feature saves copies of all open Excel files at a user-definable fixed interval. The files can be recovered if Excel closes unexpectedly, for example, during a power failure.

Concept note-2: -By default, all Excel workbook’s changes are saved automatically in every 10 minutes. This is called as AutoRecover information. You can configure the time interval to specify how often the AutoRecover information is saved.

Concept note-3: -To do this, click “File” on the ribbon, go to the “Info” section, and choose “Manage Workbook”. Choose “Recover Unsaved Workbooks” to display and recover unsaved Excel files. Excel saves unsaved files to the folder C:
AppData. You can also access the files from this folder.

Concept note-4: -AutoSave Vs AutoRecover AutoSave allows you to save your work in real-time when you save your Excel files in OneDrive or SharePoint. On the contrary, ‘Save AutoRecover Information’ is an option that automatically saves your work every 10 minutes (or whatever time period you specify).

Concept note-5: -By default the AutoRecover feature is set to automatically save changes to your workbook every 10 minutes. You can shorten or lengthen this interval as you like. Here you can also change Excel AutoRecover file location and specify AutoRecover exceptions.
