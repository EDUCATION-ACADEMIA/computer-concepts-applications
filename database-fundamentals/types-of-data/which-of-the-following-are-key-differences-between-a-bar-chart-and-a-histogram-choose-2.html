Which of the following are key differences between a bar chart and a histogram? (choose 2)

(A) ** Histograms are for numerical data and bar charts are for categorical

(B) Histograms are for categorical data and bar charts are for numerical

(C) ** A bar chart has gaps between each coloumn, a histogram does not

(D) A bar chart and histogram can be both used for categorical and numerical data.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What are the Differences Between Bar Chart and Histogram? The bar graph is the graphical representation of categorical data. A histogram is the graphical representation of quantitative data. There is equal space between each pair of consecutive bars.

Concept note-2: -Histograms visualize quantitative data or numerical data, whereas bar charts display categorical variables. In most instances, the numerical data in a histogram will be continuous (having infinite values).

Concept note-3: -Key Differences Between Histogram and Bar graph A histogram represents the frequency distribution of continuous variables. Conversely, a bar graph is a diagrammatic comparison of discrete variables. Histogram presents numerical data whereas bar graph shows categorical data.

Concept note-4: -A histogram is a representation of continuous data that are grouped into classes. There are no gaps between the bars in a histogram because the classes are continuous.

Concept note-5: -A histogram has an appearance similar to a vertical bar chart, but there are no gaps between the bars. Generally, a histogram will have bars of equal width.
