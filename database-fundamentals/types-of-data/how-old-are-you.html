How old are you?

(A) Categorical

(B) ** Numerical

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The short answer: Age is a quantitative variable because it represents a measurable quantity. What is this? For example, if someone is 35 years old then we know they are 5 years older than a 30 year-old but 5 years younger than a 40-year old.

Concept note-2: -Age can be both nominal and ordinal data depending on the question types. I.e “How old are you” is used to collect nominal data while “Are you the firstborn or What position are you in your family” is used to collect ordinal data. Age becomes ordinal data when there’s some sort of order to it.

Concept note-3: -Age – age of an individual is counted under numerical data since it can take countable numeric values.

Concept note-4: -Mondal[1] suggests that age can be viewed as a discrete variable because it is commonly expressed as an integer in units of years with no decimal to indicate days and presumably, hours, minutes, and seconds.
