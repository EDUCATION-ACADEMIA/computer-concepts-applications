Worksheet

(A) A grid of rows and columns into which you enter text data and numerical data.

(B) A cell that is selected.

(C) ** An Excel term for a spreadsheet.

(D) A feature that enables you to repeat the same data in a column or row.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A worksheet (also known as a spreadsheet) consists of cells in which you can enter and calculate data. The cells are organized into columns and rows. A worksheet is always stored in a workbook. A workbook can contain many worksheeks.

Concept note-2: -Worksheet: A worksheet is the grid of columns and rows that information is inputted into. In many spreadsheet applications (such as Microsoft Excel) one file–called a workbook–can contain several worksheets. Worksheets can be named using the sheet tabs of the bottom of the spreadsheet window.

Concept note-3: -What is a spreadsheet? A spreadsheet is a computer program that can capture, display and manipulate data arranged in rows and columns. Spreadsheets are one of the most popular tools available with personal computers. A spreadsheet is generally designed to hold numerical data and short text strings.

Concept note-4: -The word “spreadsheet” came from “spread” in its sense of a newspaper or magazine item (text or graphics) that covers two facing pages, extending across the centerfold and treating the two pages as one large page.

Concept note-5: -A spreadsheet or worksheet is a file made of rows and columns that help sort, organize, and arrange data efficiently, and calculate numerical data.
