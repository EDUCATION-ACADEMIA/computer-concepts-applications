At least how many characters should your password have?

(A) 2

(B) 7

(C) ** 8

(D) 5

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Passwords should be a minimum of eight characters in length. Longer passwords are more secure and should contain upper and lower case characters, numbers, and special characters. This provides a character set of 95 possible characters.

Concept note-2: -Answer: 8 characters is between 1 words and 2 words with spaces included in the character count. If spaces are not included in the character count, then 8 characters is between 1 words and 3 words.

Concept note-3: -With computer power increasing all the time, the minimum required password length is a moving target that’s only going to go up. But for today’s computing power, eight is the minimum number of characters that the NIST recommends.

Concept note-4: -Passwords must have at least 8 characters and contain at least two of the following: uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols.
