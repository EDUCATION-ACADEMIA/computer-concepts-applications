In a school system, the relationship between teachers and pupils would be:

(A) One-to-One

(B) One-to-Many

(C) ** Many-to-Many

(D) Many-to-One

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A student-teacher relationship in the classroom is a positive relationship between the teacher and the student in efforts to gain trust and respect from each other. This relationship may consist of getting to know your students better, providing choice and encouraging the students to become stronger learners everyday.

Concept note-2: -When it comes to relationship between student and teacher, the most appropriate answer here would be option (b), one to many, because; in a class there are several students and one teacher. This teacher is the sole member and has to take care of the needs of each and every student in the classroom.

Concept note-3: -Pupil-teacher ratio is calculated by dividing the number of students at the specified level of education by the number of teachers at the same level of education.

Concept note-4: -Seventy percent of the talk time should be student talk time. The remaining 30 percent should be teacher talk time.
