USING MICROSOFT WORD

CREATING HEADERS FOOTERS IN MICROSOFT WORD

[SOURCES]
The ____ is the top triangle at the 0” mark on the horizontal ruler.

(A) hanging indent marker

(B) left indent marker

(C) ** first line indent marker

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Move your mouse cursor to the two triangles on the left of the horizontal ruler. These are indent markers. The top triangle represents the first line indent marker. It is known as the left indent marker.

Concept note-2: -Also called automatic page break. The top triangle at the 0″ mark on the ruler that marks the first line indent of a paragraph.

Concept note-3: -Indent markers on the horizontal ruler always indicate the default indent settings of the document. When you apply highlighting to text, the highlighting is only visible on the screen and does not print. You must apply text wrapping to a graphic in order to move it independently of text.

Concept note-4: -The indent markers are located to the left of the horizontal ruler, and they provide several indenting options: First-line indent marker. adjusts the first-line indent.
