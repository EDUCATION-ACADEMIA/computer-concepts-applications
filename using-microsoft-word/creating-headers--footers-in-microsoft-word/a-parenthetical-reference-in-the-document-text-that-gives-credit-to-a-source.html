USING MICROSOFT WORD

CREATING HEADERS FOOTERS IN MICROSOFT WORD

[SOURCES]
A parenthetical reference in the document text that gives credit to a source

(A) Endnote

(B) Footnote

(C) Bullet

(D) ** Citation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A parenthetical citation gives credit in parentheses to a source that you’re quoting or paraphrasing. It contains information such as the author’s name, the publication date, and the page number(s) if relevant. Parenthetical citations are used in many citation styles, including MLA, APA, and Chicago.

Concept note-2: -Parenthetical citations are citations to original sources that appear in the text of your paper. This allows the reader to see immediately where your information comes from, and it saves you the trouble of having to make footnotes or endnotes.

Concept note-3: -A citation is a parenthetical reference in the document text that gives credit to the source for a quotation or other information used in a document.

Concept note-4: -MLA parenthetical citation style uses the author’s last name and a page number; for example: (Field 122). When you incorporate a direct quotation into a sentence, you must cite the source.

Concept note-5: -The in-text citation should occur in the sentence where the cited material has been used: Signal phrase reference (author’s name) appears within the sentence with page number in parentheses at the end of the sentence. Full parenthetical reference (author last name and page number) appears at the end of the sentence.
