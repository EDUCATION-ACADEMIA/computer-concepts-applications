USING MICROSOFT WORD

MICROSOFT WORD

[SOURCES]
The amount of space before and after each paragraph in a document is known as:

(A) ** Paragraph spacing

(B) Sentence spacing.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A first-line indent is the most common way to signal the start of a new paragraph. The other common way is with space between paragraphs. First-line indents and space between paragraphs have the same relationship as belts and suspenders.

Concept note-2: -The traditional term for line spacing is leading (rhymes with bedding ), so named because traditional print shops put strips of lead between lines of type to increase vertical space. Sometimes you see this term in typesetting software. For most text, the optimal line spacing is between 120% and 145% of the point size.

Concept note-3: -Line Spacing: Line spacing refers to the amount of white space between lines of text in a paragraph. Commonly used line spacing settings are single spaced and double spaced.

Concept note-4: -Double space: Your entire essay should be double spaced, with no single spacing anywhere and no extra spacing anywhere. There should not be extra spaces between paragraphs. Margins: According to the MLA, your essay should have a one-inch margin on the top, bottom, left, and right.
