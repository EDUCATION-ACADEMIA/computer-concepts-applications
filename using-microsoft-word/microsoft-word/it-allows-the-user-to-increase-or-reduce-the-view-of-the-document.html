USING MICROSOFT WORD

MICROSOFT WORD

[SOURCES]
It allows the user to increase or reduce the view of the document.

(A) Split

(B) Print Preview

(C) ** Zoom

(D) Page Breaks

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Choose a particular zoom setting You can choose how much of a document, presentation, or worksheet you view on the screen. On the View tab, in the Zoom group, click Zoom 100%. This returns the view to 100% zoom. On the View tab, in the Zoom group, click One Page, Multiple Pages, or Page Width.

Concept note-2: -From the top of the screen, swipe down to find quick settings. Magnification shortcut. Switch to full screen or Switch to partial screen. Move the slider to increase or decrease zoom.

Concept note-3: -Magnify or reduce the view of your image with the Zoom tool. Select the Zoom tool from the toolbar. In the tool options bar, choose the Zoom In option to magnify your view or the Zoom Out option to reduce the image view. Click the area you want to zoom in or out in your image.

Concept note-4: -In Microsoft Office products, like Word, Excel, and PowerPoint, a zoom bar or zoom slider is available for zooming in and out of documents quickly and easily. The zoom slider is usually in the lower right corner of the software window and should resemble the picture on this page.
