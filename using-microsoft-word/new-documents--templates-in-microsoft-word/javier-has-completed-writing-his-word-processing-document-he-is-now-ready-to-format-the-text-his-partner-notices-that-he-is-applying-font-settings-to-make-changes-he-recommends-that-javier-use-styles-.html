USING MICROSOFT WORD

NEW DOCUMENTS TEMPLATES IN MICROSOFT WORD

[SOURCES]
Javier has completed writing his word-processing document. He is now ready to format the text. His partner notices that he is applying font settings to make changes. He recommends that Javier use styles to format his text instead. Why?

(A) He cannot change font settings after he applies them and saves his document, but he can change style settings.

(B) There are more style options available to use in formatting text than there are font settings.

(C) Styles is the only text formatting feature you can use after you have written the text.

(D) ** When applying a style to text, he simply needs to make a change to the style and all text with that style applied will be similarly changed.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A Word processor is a computer program that provides for input, editing, formatting, and output of text. It includes some additional features such as creating, editing, saving, and printing documents.

Concept note-2: -Instead of using direct formatting, use styles to format your document so you can quickly and easily apply a set of formatting choices consistently throughout your document.

Concept note-3: -Hence, the term formatting is associated with changing the appearance of a document in word processing.
