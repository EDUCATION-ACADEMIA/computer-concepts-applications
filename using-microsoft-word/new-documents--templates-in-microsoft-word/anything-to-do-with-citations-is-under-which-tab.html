USING MICROSOFT WORD

NEW DOCUMENTS TEMPLATES IN MICROSOFT WORD

[SOURCES]
Anything to do with citations is under which tab.

(A) ** References

(B) Layout

(C) Insert

(D) View

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -On the References tab, in the Citations & Bibliography group, click the arrow next to Style and click the style that you want to use for the citation and source. For example, social sciences documents usually use the MLA or APA styles for citations and sources.

Concept note-2: -The References Tab allows you to now create a table of contents, footnotes, citations, cross-references, select MLA or APA or ISO formats and so on. You can also turn your Works Cited into a collection of records and use them in the future.

Concept note-3: -Put your cursor at the end of the text you want to cite. Go to References > Style, and choose a citation style. Select Insert Citation. Choose Add New Source and fill out the information about your source.

Concept note-4: -The References tab allows you add annotations to your document, such as footnotes and citations.
