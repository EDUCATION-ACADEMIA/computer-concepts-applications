What happens when you scroll in a Word document?

(A) ** The display of text changes but the insertion point does not move.

(B) The display of text changes and the insertion point moves.

(C) The text is not selected but the insertion point moves.

(D) The text is selected but the insertion point does not move.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Simply click the location in the text where you want to place it. Arrow keys: You can also use the arrow keys on your keyboard to move the insertion point. The left and right arrow keys will move between adjacent characters on the same line, while the up and down arrows will move between paragraph lines.

Concept note-2: -With the text box selected, choose Text Box>Position>More Layout Options and check the Lock anchor option.

Concept note-3: -The insertion point automatically responds when you scroll through a document using the scroll bars. When the mouse pointer is in the text area, it is referred to as an I-beam. Tapping the [Home] key always moves the insertion point to the top of the document.

Concept note-4: -Expert-Verified Answer Here rest of the options are eliminated and the right answer of the given question is option CTRL + Down Arrow.
