How does a computer system work?

(A) ** Input-Process-Output

(B) Process-Output-Input

(C) Output-Input-Process

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Input-the information entered into a computer system, examples include: typed text, mouse clicks, etc. Processing-the process of transforming input information into and output. Output â€“ the visual, auditory, or tactile perceptions provided by the computer after processing the provided information.

Concept note-2: -A computer, a digital information-processing machine, works by changing information into binary numbers (ones and zeros) and then using simple mathematics to make decisions about how to rearrange those numbers into words or actions.

Concept note-3: -Input devices accept data in a form that the computer can use; they then send the data to the processing unit. The processor, more formally known as the central processing unit (CPU), has the electronic circuitry that manipulates input data into the information people want.

Concept note-4: -It takes information or data from one end, store it to process, and finally, after completing the processing, it output the result on the other hand. The information it takes at one end is known as Computer Input, and the result that it provides after processing is known as Computer Output.

Concept note-5: -The inputs represent the flow of data and materials into the process from the outside. The processing step includes all tasks required to effect a transformation of the inputs. The outputs are the data and materials flowing out of the transformation process.
