Why is WiFi encryption more of an issue than with Ethernet?

(A) Ethernet is more modern

(B) WiFi is more common

(C) ** WiFi signals travel through the air

(D) More people are on phones these days

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In general, a wireless connection is less secure than a wired network since its communication and data signals are transmitted through the air. Since connections travel via radio waves, they can be easily intercepted if there are no proper encryption technologies in place.

Concept note-2: -In general, Ethernet can be considered more secure than Wi-Fi, but this can vary based on the security measures in place. Ethernet networks are typically physically secured, meaning that access to the network requires physical access to the connected devices or to the wired infrastructure.

Concept note-3: -A WiFi connection is more susceptible to interference from electrical devices or physical objects that can block the signal. An Ethernet connection is consequently more reliable, as it is insulated from interference and crosstalk and unaffected by the presence of physical objects.

With wireless networks, the Wi-Fi signal is broadcasted outside of the building, leaving it open to the public and potential hackers.

Concept note-5: -Ethernet uses physical cables and can deliver faster speeds, less interference and sometimes better security. Wi-Fi uses signals sent through the air, making it easier to move devices around your home, and a Wi-Fi connection is secure and speedy enough for many purposes.
