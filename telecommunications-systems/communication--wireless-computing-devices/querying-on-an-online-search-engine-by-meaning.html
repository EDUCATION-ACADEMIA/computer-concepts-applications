(A) Mobile search

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -So in terms of search, a semantic search engine is doing a study of meaning, focusing on the meanings of search terms being entered. Essentially, semantic search works by drawing links between words and phrases; it’s able to interpret digital content in a more “human” way.

Concept note-2: -What Is Semantic Search? Semantic search describes a search engine’s attempt to generate the most accurate SERP results possible by understanding based on searcher intent, query context, and the relationship between words. This is important as: People say things and query things in different ways, languages, and tones.

Concept note-3: -Here, Google uses semantic search to understand the relationship between words to offer results. See the following images for context. It is imperative to note that Google penalizes pages and URLs for optimizing for search spiders rather than users, hence the creation of BERT.

Concept note-4: -The technologies that are being used for Semantic Search by Google are Knowledge Graph, Hummingbird, RankBrain, and BERT.

Concept note-5: -DuckDuckGo: If you use Google, forget it, because unlike Google, DuckDuckGo is a feature-rich Semantic Search Engine. If you search for a term that has more than one meaning, with its disambiguation results, you will be able to select what you originally searching for.
