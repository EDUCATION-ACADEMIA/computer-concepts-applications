[SOURCES]
The main WiFi bands are divided into ____

(A) Subnets

(B) Minor Frequencies

(C) ** Channels

(D) IP Addresses

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It may be divided into eight 5 MHz channels, four 10 MHz channels, or two 20 MHz channels. This band is being utilises by the Wi-Fi 6E, E for extended as it accesses the nominally named 6 GHz band which enables channel bandwidths of 20, 40, 80 & 160 MHz to be used.

Concept note-2: -Selecting the proper Wi-Fi channel can significantly improve your Wi-Fi coverage and performance. In the 2.4 GHz band, 1, 6, and 11 are the only non-overlapping channels. Selecting one or more of these channels is an important part of setting up your network correctly.

Concept note-3: -In the United States, the 2.4 GHz band is broken up into 11 channels (1-11), each 20MHz wide. In the 5 GHz band, we have channels ranging from 36 up to 165, and in the 6 GHz band, we have Wi-Fi channels ranging from 1-233.

Concept note-4: -The most common WiFi bands are 2.4 GHz, 5 GHz, and 6 GHz, though some devices support uncommon standards like 60 Ghz for specific applications. Generally speaking, higher frequencies enable greater speeds but have reduced broadcast range.

Concept note-5: -WLAN Frequency Bands: The 802.11 working group currently documents use in five distinct frequency ranges: 2.4 GHz, 3.6 GHz, 4.9 GHz, 5 GHz, and 5.9 GHz bands. Each range is divided into a multitude of channels.
