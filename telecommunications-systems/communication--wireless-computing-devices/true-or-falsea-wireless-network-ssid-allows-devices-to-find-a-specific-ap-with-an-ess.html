True or false:A wireless network SSID allows devices to find a specific AP with an ESS

(A) True

(B) ** False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -SSID is simply the technical term for a Wi-Fi network name. When you set up a wireless home network, you give it a name to distinguish it from other networks in your neighbourhood. Youâ€™ll see this name when you connect your devices to your wireless network.

Concept note-2: -What is SSID used for? Access points and wireless router networks broadcast their SSIDs to identify themselves. Your device can connect to any SSID if the network has no security options enabled. But your device will need a password if the network is secured.

Concept note-3: -Is it recommended to set all wifi routers in the same network to the same SSID, encryption and password? Absolutely. This means (as said previously) that your device will connect to the strongest signal without you having to CHOOSE to do so.
