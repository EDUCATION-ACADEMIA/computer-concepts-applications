The ability of increased and expand the network

(A) mobility

(B) flexibility

(C) ** Scalability

(D) probability

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Scalability is the ability of a network to cope with increasing workloads in a cost-effective and sustainable way, by expanding the network’s bandwidth capacity and supporting its physical expansion to new development areas.

Concept note-2: -It often refers to increasing or decreasing resources as needed to meet the higher or lower demands of a business. Vertical (scale-up) scalability increases the capacity of hardware or software by adding resources to a physical system, such as adding processing power to a server to make it faster.

Concept note-3: -One way to improve network scalability is by increasing the capacity of your wireless network. This can be done by adding more access points or upgrading your existing ones. If you have the budget, you could also invest in a mesh network, which would give you even more flexibility and scalability.

Concept note-4: -In computing, scalability is a characteristic of computers, networks, algorithms, networking protocols, programs and applications. An example is a search engine, which must support increasing numbers of users, and the number of topics it indexes.

Concept note-5: -Network scalability provides the flexibility you need to grow your business over time. If your network is scalable, you’ll never need to think twice about pushing for an increase in orders, or upping the service you offer your customers.
