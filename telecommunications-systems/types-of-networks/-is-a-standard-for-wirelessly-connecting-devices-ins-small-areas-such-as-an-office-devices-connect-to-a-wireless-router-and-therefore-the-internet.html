TYPES OF NETWORKS

[SOURCES]
____ is a standard for wirelessly connecting devices ins small areas, such as an office. Devices connect to a wireless router and therefore the Internet.

(A) ** WiFi

(B) Ethernet

(C) WiMAX

(D) Fiber Optic Cable

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A wireless access point connects devices to the network wirelessly, using radio frequencies in the 900 MHz and 2.4, 3.6, 5, and 60 GHz frequency bands.

Concept note-2: -At present, the IEEE 802.11b/g standard is widely accepted throughout the industry and provides the necessary balance of range, network throughput, and support for device mobility to effectively serve most needs of the University community.

Concept note-3: -A router is a device that provides Wi-Fi and is typically connected to a modem. It sends information from the internet to personal devices like computers, phones, and tablets. These internet-connected devices in your home make up your Local Area Network (LAN).
