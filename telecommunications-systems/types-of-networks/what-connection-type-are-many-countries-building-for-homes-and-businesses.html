TYPES OF NETWORKS

[SOURCES]
What connection type are many countries building for homes and businesses?

(A) Ethernet

(B) Wi-fi

(C) Bluetooth

(D) ** Fibre optics

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Fiber optic internet is a data connection carried by a cable filled with thin glass or plastic fibers. Data travels through them as beams of light pulsed in a pattern. Fiber optic internet speeds are about 20 times faster than regular cable at 1 Gbps.

Concept note-2: -Fiber optical networks use signals encoded onto light to transmit information among various nodes of a telecommunication network. They operate from the limited range of a local-area network (LAN) or over a wide-area network (WAN).

Concept note-3: -Fiber-optic is delivered via a cable, either from underground or an aerial power pole, that goes right into your house. This is called Fiber to the Home, which is just what you want if you can get it. Your internet service provider will bring it right to your door. Of course, it’s what’s inside the cable that counts.

Concept note-4: -Premises cabling distances are short so attenuation of the fiber is of less concern, although bandwidth can be a major issue with gigabit networks and faster. Most premises networks use multimode fiber since it uses inexpensive sources like LEDs and VCSELs.
