A network that covers a larger geographical area than a LAN, but is usually as large as a WAN.

(A) ** Metropolitan Area Network (MAN)

(B) Wide Area Network (WAN)

(C) Local Area Network (LAN)

(D) Bus Topology

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A metropolitan area network (MAN) is a computer network that connects computers within a metropolitan area, which could be a single large city, multiple cities and towns, or any given large area with multiple buildings. A MAN is larger than a local area network (LAN) but smaller than a wide area network (WAN).

Concept note-2: -metropolitan area network (MAN) A metropolitan area network (MAN) is a computer network that is larger than a single building local area network (LAN) but is located in a single geographic area that is smaller than a wide area network (WAN).

Concept note-3: -A LAN, abbreviated from Local Area Network, is a network that covers a small geographical area such as homes, offices, and groups of buildings. Whereas a WAN, abbreviated from Wide Area Network, is a network that covers larger geographical areas that can span the globe.

Concept note-4: -Answer and Explanation: The Correct option is B-Wide Area Network. WAN (Wide area network) is defined as the computer network which spans over a large geographical area like states and countries. This network is larger than LAN (Local area network).

Concept note-5: -A metropolitan area network is a network that covers a larger geographic area by interconnecting a different LAN to form a larger network. Government agencies use MAN to connect to the citizens and private industries. In MAN, various LANs are connected to each other through a telephone exchange line.
