TELECOMMUNICATIONS SYSTEMS

THE COMPONENTS OF A TELECOMMUNICATIONS SYSTEM

[SOURCES]
The passing on, or transfer, of information

(A) ** transmit

(B) encode

(C) signal

(D) encrypt

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Communication is simply the act of transferring information from one place, person or group to another.

Concept note-2: -The transmission model of communication describes communication as a one-way, linear process in which a sender encodes a message and transmits it through a channel to a receiver who decodes it. The transmission of the message many be disrupted by environmental or semantic noise.

Concept note-3: -Communication has been defined in various ways. In one definition that frequently crops up in the literature of communication, communication has been described as the “transmission of information” or the “transfer of meaning.”

Concept note-4: -The sender is the person who transmits the message. The person who transmits a message to the other person is called the sender, and the person who receives the message is called the receiver. With communication there is two-way traffic.

Concept note-5: -Digital and analog signals are transmitted through electromagnetic waves. Changes in frequency and amplitude create the music you listen to or images that you see on a screen. Analog signals are composed of continuous waves that can have any values for frequency and amplitude.
