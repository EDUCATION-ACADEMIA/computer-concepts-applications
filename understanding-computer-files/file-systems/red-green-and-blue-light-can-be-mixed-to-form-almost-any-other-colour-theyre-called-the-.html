Red, Green, and Blue light can be mixed to form (almost) any other colour. They’re called the ____

(A) ** Additive primary colours

(B) Additive secondary colours

(C) Subtractive primary colours

(D) Subtractive secondary colours

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -All other colors of the visible light spectrum can be produced by properly adding different combinations of these three colors. Moreover, adding equal amounts of red, green, and blue light produces white light and, therefore, these colors are also often described as the primary additive colors.

Concept note-2: -Detailed Solution. The correct answer is White. The white colour of light is formed when red, blue, and green colours of light meet in equal proportion.

Concept note-3: -Primary Additive Colors Light is perceived as white by humans when all three cone cell types are simultaneously stimulated by equal amounts of red, green, and blue light. Because the addition of these three colors yields white light, the colors red, green, and blue are termed the primary additive colors.

Concept note-4: -Additive secondaries For the human eye, good primary colors of light are red, green, and blue. Combining lights of these colors produces a large range of visible colors. Combining RGB colors means adding light (thus the term “additive color"), and the combinations are brighter.

Concept note-5: -Red (R) Green (G) and blue (B) colors are known as primary colors of light. because of mixing these three colors we are able to make all possible colors.
