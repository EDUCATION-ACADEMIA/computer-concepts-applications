(A) ** Object-Oriented Programming

(B) Object-Oriented Languages

(C) Object-Oriented Procedures

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Object-oriented programming (OOP) is a style of programming characterized by the identification of classes of objects closely linked with the methods (functions) with which they are associated. It also includes ideas of inheritance of attributes and methods.

Concept note-2: -According to the TIOBE index, the top twenty OOP languages are Java, C++, C#, Python, R, PHP, Visual Basic.NET, JavaScript, Ruby, Perl, Object Pascal, Objective-C, Dart, Swift, Scala, Kotlin, Common Lisp, MATLAB, and Smalltalk.
