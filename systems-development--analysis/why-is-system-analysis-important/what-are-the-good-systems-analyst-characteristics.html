What are the Good systems analyst characteristics

(A) ** Impertinence, Impartiality, Relaxing constraints, Attention to details, and Reframing

(B) Important, Imergency, Ralation contraints, Attention to Details and Reframe

(C) Impertinence, Impartiality, Relaxing Consraints, Action to Details and Restructure

(D) None of the abouve

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The main requirements for a systems analyst are a solid working knowledge of computer systems and of information technology. Strong communication skills are also a must. It is a management position, so you must be comfortable and capable of efficiently managing other people.

Concept note-2: -Fluent language skills and written communication abilities are important to succeed as a system analyst. An analyst should know what IT technologies are being used in order to find system solutions, what new potential results can be accomplished across existing systems, and what the latest technology offers.

Concept note-3: -Required Skills/Abilities: Excellent verbal and written communication skills. Excellent interpersonal skills with a proven ability to collaborate with a team. Excellent analytical and problem-solving skills. Extensive knowledge of computer systems and programming.

Concept note-4: -The analyst plays many roles, sometimes balancing several at the same time. The three primary roles of the systems analyst are: consultant, supporting expert, and agent of change."
