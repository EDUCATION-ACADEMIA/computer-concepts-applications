After solving a problem on a computer, a technician checks the event log to ensure that there are no new error messages. At which step of the troubleshooting process is this action taking place?

(A) Document the findings.

(B) Determine an exact cause.

(C) Establish a theory of probable cause.

(D) ** Verify the solution and full system functionality.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Document the findings. Answers Explanation & Hints: In this situation, the probable cause has been identified and the exact cause has been acted upon. Documenting the findings is the last action in the process and happens after checking that the problem has been solved by the solution implemented.

Concept note-2: -What is the next step in the troubleshooting process that the technician should perform? Verify the solution and confirm full system functionality.

Concept note-3: -In which step of the troubleshooting process would a technician have to do more research on the Internet or within the computer manual in order to solve a problem? Establish a plan of action to resolve the problem and implement the solution.

Concept note-4: -7. When troubleshooting a computer, where can a technician find updated information abouterrors caused by the system, the user, or the software? Event Viewer*Device Managerthe BIOSvendor diagnostic toolsExplanation:Information about errors caused by the system, user, or software can be found inEvent Viewer.

Concept note-5: -During what step in the troubleshooting process does the technician demonstrate to the customer how the solution corrected the problem? Document the findings, actions, and outcomes.
