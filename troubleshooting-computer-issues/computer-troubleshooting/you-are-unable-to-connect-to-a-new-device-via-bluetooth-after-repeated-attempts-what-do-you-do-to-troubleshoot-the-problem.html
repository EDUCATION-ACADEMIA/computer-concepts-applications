TROUBLESHOOTING COMPUTER ISSUES

COMPUTER TROUBLESHOOTING

[SOURCES]
You are unable to connect to a new device via Bluetooth after repeated attempts. What do you do to troubleshoot the problem?

(A) ** Move the devices closer together or change their relative orientations to each other

(B) Turn off Bluetooth on the device

(C) Return your device to the manufacturer for repair

(D) Uninstall Bluetooth support for your device and reinstall it

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -You are unable to connect to a new device via Bluetooth after repeated attempts. What do you do to troubleshoot the problem? Move the devices closer together or change their relative orientations to each other.

Concept note-2: -Try these steps first Turn your Bluetooth accessory off and back on again. Make sure that your Bluetooth accessory is on and fully charged or connected to power. If your accessory uses batteries, see if they need to be replaced.

Concept note-3: -Turn off Bluetooth, wait a few seconds, then turn it back on. Remove the Bluetooth device, then add it again: Select Start, then select Settings > Devices > Bluetooth & other devices .. In Bluetooth, select the device you’re having problems connecting to, and then select Remove device > Yes.

Concept note-4: -Power Bluetooth off and back on A soft reset of Bluetooth can sometimes resolve an issue. With phones, an easy way to do this is by going into and out of airplane mode. For other devices, turn off the power and restart them.

Concept note-5: -Make sure Bluetooth is turned on. Touch and hold Bluetooth . In the list of paired devices, tap a paired but unconnected device. When your phone and the Bluetooth device are connected, the device shows as “Connected."
