TROUBLESHOOTING COMPUTER ISSUES

COMPUTER TROUBLESHOOTING

[SOURCES]
Troubleshooting is always a process of

(A) random act to get the solution you want

(B) trying different things

(C) ** trial and error

(D) not knowing what you are doing

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -No matter what’s causing the issue, troubleshooting will always be a process of trial and error-in some cases, you may need to use several different approaches before you can find a solution; other problems may be easy to fix.

Concept note-2: -A basic troubleshooting strategy: Recording the symptoms of the problem. Re-creating the problem. Eliminating possible causes.

Concept note-3: -Troubleshooting is a systematic approach to solving a problem. The goal of troubleshooting is to determine why something does not work as expected and explain how to resolve the problem. The first step in the troubleshooting process is to describe the problem completely.

Concept note-4: -Expert-Verified Answer The process of troubleshooting does not include copying an error. Concerning the troubleshooting procedure: Troubleshooting is a systematic method for resolving a problem. Troubleshooting is the process of determining why something isn’t working properly and determining how to fix it.

Concept note-5: -The first step in the troubleshooting process is to identify the problem.
