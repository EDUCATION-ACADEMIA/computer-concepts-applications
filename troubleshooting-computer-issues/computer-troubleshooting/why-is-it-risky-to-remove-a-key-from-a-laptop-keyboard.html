Why is it risky to remove a key from a laptop keyboard?

(A) ** An easy-to-break plastic hinge holds it on.

(B) It is easy to damage interface wires when removing keys.

(C) You can easily damage the liquid crystal display (LCD) screen if you slip when removing a key.

(D) If you touch a motherboard component, you could short it.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Removing your laptop keys is possible but should be done as infrequently as possible to prevent the keys from malfunctioning. However, if something is spilled on the laptop, removing some or all keys to clean underneath them will be necessary.

Concept note-2: -To get keycaps off, you can pry them up with a screwdriver or spudger. You really shouldnâ€™t, though. To avoid damage, buy a wire keycap puller.

Concept note-3: -The partial working of the keys of the laptop means that there are dust particles around the keypad that are making hurdles to communicating the message of pressing and releasing keys. To fix it, remove the keycaps of the specific keys carefully and then remove the key retainer.
