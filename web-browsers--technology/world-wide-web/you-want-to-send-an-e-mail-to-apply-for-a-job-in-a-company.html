You want to send an e-mail to apply for a job in a company.

(A) Attractive colors

(B) Smilies

(C) Fancy quotations

(D) ** Formal Salutation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -If not, you may determine who the hiring manager is by researching their title on the company’s LinkedIn page. If that information is not available, keep your salutation generic with a simple “To Whom It May Concern” or “Dear [Department] Hiring Team.”

Concept note-2: -Use a generic salutation, such as Dear Hiring Manager, Dear Recruiting Manager or Dear Human Resources Professional. (Avoid To Whom It May Concern; it is antiquated.) Another option is to write Greetings, which is somewhat informal but polite.

Concept note-3: -Use “Hello, ” or “Dear, ” followed by their first and last name. If the job description includes the hiring manager’s name, or if you’ve managed to figure it out through research (which we’ll cover below), an easy greeting uses a full name with a “Dear” or “Hello” before it.
