What is one difference between online and face-to-face communication?

(A) People tend to be more honest online

(B) Miscommunications happen more frequently than face-to-face

(C) ** Online, you can not tell the tone of voice

(D) Body language is more important in online communication

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Nonverbal cues are where we see the biggest difference between the two forms of communication. In face-to-face conversations, we can identify the tone of voice and use body language to understand the content of messages. Online communication does not have that luxury.

Concept note-2: -Computer-mediated communication refers to all communication that takes place via computer, whereas face-to-face communication is just that, communication that takes place with two people actually speaking.

Concept note-3: -Face-to-face interaction is social communication carried out without any mediating technology. It is defined as the mutual influence of individualsâ€™ direct physical presence with their body language and verbal language.

Concept note-4: -Online communication refers to the sharing of information or communication over a computer network (the Internet).
