Why is it important to have a good and strong password?

(A) ** That way it will difficult for others to steal your identity or log on to your email/website.

(B) That way the website or email service provider allow us to change whenever we want.

(C) That way the link to your password will not be broken.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In a dictionary attack, sophistiated hackers program a malicious software to run through hundreds, if not thousands, of variations of common words or passwords in an attempt to guess your password. The weaker your password the easier it is for an attacker to guess your password and access your account.

Concept note-2: -Passwords provide the first line of defense against unauthorized access to your computer and personal information. The stronger your password, the more protected your computer will be from hackers and malicious software.

Concept note-3: -Using a unique password for each account means that even in the event of a data breach in one of the services you use, your other accounts are not at risk.

Concept note-4: -Creating a strong and secure password can reduce the risk of cybercriminals guessing your password and accessing sensitive data. Compromised passwords caused 80% of all data breaches in 2019, resulting in financial losses for both businesses and consumers.

Hackers use computers to try various combinations of letters, numbers, and symbols in search of the right password. Modern computers can crack short passwords consisting of only letters and numbers in mere moments.
