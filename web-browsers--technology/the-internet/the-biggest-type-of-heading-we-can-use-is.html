The biggest type of heading we can use is

(A) ** <h1>

(B) <h2>

(C) <h3>

(D) <h4>

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Correct HTML tag for the largest heading is <h1>. In an HTML page, the <h1> to <h6> tags are used to define headers. HTML header tags distinguish a pageâ€™s headers (h1) and sub-headings (h2-h6) from the rest of the content. The largest heading is defined by <h1>, while the smallest heading is defined by <h6>.

Concept note-2: -Traditionally they are like the main heading (H1), the major section headings (H2), minor points (H3) etc. The H1 should almost always be the largest text on the page. So an H1 is always BIG, H2 slightly less so.

Concept note-3: -The H1 heading should be the largest heading on the page. The H2 heading is a little smaller than the H1, and in turn the H3 heading is smaller than then H2 heading and so on.

Concept note-4: -H2 heading tag It defines the second-level headings on your webpage. Like an H1 tag, an H2 tag also appears larger than the rest of your main body text. However, H2 tags are always slightly smaller than your H1 tag, so that it does not compete with your main heading.

Concept note-5: -The <h1> to <h6> HTML elements represent six levels of section headings. <h1> is the highest section level and <h6> is the lowest.
