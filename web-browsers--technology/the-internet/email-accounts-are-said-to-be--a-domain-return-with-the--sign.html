Email accounts are said to be ____ a domain return with the ____ sign.

(A) hypens, -

(B) asterisk, *

(C) ** at, @

(D) hashtag, #

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -On the Internet, (pronounced “at” or “at sign” or “address sign") is the symbol in an E-mail address that separates the name of the user from the user’s Internet address, as in this hypothetical e-mail address example: msmuffettuffet.org.

Concept note-2: -The last part of the email address is the domain that comes right after the at sign. The domain consists of the name of the email server and the top-level domain. So, for example, if we continue with the example of Emmaone.com, one is the name of the email server, and .com is the top-level domain.

Concept note-3: -An email domain is the part of an email address that comes after the “” symbol. If you use Gmail, for example, the domain name for your email address is gmail.com. Other common email domains you’ve probably seen include yahoo.com, hotmail.com, and outlook.com.

Concept note-4: -Components of an email address Username: part of the email before the symbol is the username and is usually the name of the person who owns or manages the specific mailbox. symbol: separates the username and domain name.
