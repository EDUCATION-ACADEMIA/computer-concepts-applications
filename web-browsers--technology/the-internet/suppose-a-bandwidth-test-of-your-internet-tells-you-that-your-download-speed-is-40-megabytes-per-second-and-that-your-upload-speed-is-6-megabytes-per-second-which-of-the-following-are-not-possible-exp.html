THE INTERNET

Suppose a bandwidth test of your Internet tells you that your download speed is 40 megabytes per second and that your upload speed is 6 megabytes per second. Which of the following are NOT possible explanations for this difference?

(A) The ISP controls this so sites download as fast as possible for users

(B) More information is downloaded per second that uploaded per second

(C) ** A download has greater latency than an upload

(D) An upload has less latency than a download

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -For a 40 Mbps download speed, you can use an upload speed of up to 4 Mbps. Here are the upload speeds required for video conferencing on different platforms: Skype HD calls: 1.5 Mbps. Zoom one-on-one HQ calls: 0.6 Mbps.

Concept note-2: -MBps indicates the real-time download and upload speed of a particular activity such as gaming, video streaming, images download, etc. Say your internet connectivity is 100 Mbps. Then the download speed will be = 100 Mbps/8 = 12.5 MBps.

Concept note-3: -Megabits per second (Mbps) are units of measurement for network bandwidth and throughput. They are used to show how fast a network or internet connection is. Each Mbps represents the capacity to transfer 1 million bits each second, or roughly one small photo per second. It may also be expressed as Mbit/s or Mb/s.

Concept note-4: -Most broadband Internet connections have dedicated bandwidth for both uploads and downloads. If your Internet plan is described as “100 Mbps up/10 Mbps down”, for example, you actually have a total of 110 Mbps available, though 10 Mbps is dedicated to uploads.
