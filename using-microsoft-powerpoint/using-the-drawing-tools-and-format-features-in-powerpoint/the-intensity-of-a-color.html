The intensity of a color.

(A) ** saturation

(B) constrain

(C) tone

(D) aspect rato

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -SATURATION (intensity)-Chrome or saturation which indicates the purity of color is known as the intensity. Colors which are mixed and have ultimate degree of vividness are said to exhibit ‘high intensity’. Two colors may have same hue value but yet differ in color strength.

Concept note-2: -Saturation is also referred to as “intensity” and “chroma.” It refers to the dominance of hue in the color. On the outer edge of the hue wheel are the ‘pure’ hues. As you move into the center of the wheel, the hue we are using to describe the color dominates less and less.

Concept note-3: -Color intensity refers to the brightness or dullness of a color. The brighter a color is, the more intense it’s said to be. Using our samples of yellow and gray, it’s easy to see that the yellow is more intense than the gray. 100% intensity is a color-any color-without any white or black mixed in.
