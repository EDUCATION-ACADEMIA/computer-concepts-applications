What are the two default dimensions for Slide Size?

(A) ** Standard(4:3) and Wide Screen (16:9)

(B) Standard(3:4) and Wide Screen (9:16)

(C) Standard(9:16) and Wide Screen (3:4)

(D) Standard(16:9) and Wide Screen (4:3)

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Overhead provides an aspect ratio very close to the standard 4:3 slide size. There are two options for 16:9 aspect ratios: On-screen Show (16:9) sets the slide dimensions to 10 in x 5.625 in. Widescreen sets the dimensions to 13.333 in x 7.5 in.

Concept note-2: -In the 4:3 aspect ratio, the dimensions are 1024 x 768 pixels (10.67″ x 8″); the height is 3/4 of the width. Back in the Ye Olde Days of PowerPoint, it was your only choice.

Concept note-3: -“4:3” and “16:9” refers to the aspect ratio of the slide. But the actual size of a slide with the same aspect ratio can differ. A 4:3 slide is 33% wider than it is high. A 16:9 slide is 78% wider than it is high.

Concept note-4: -If you work extensively with iPads and presentations in the field then 4:3 is the format for you. In most cases, however, 16:9 remains the aspect ratio of choice for PowerPoint presentations.

Concept note-5: -Standard (4:3) slides have a size of 10 x 7.5 inches or 25.4 x 19.05 cm. Widescreen (16:9) is the default slide size in Microsoft PowerPoint 2013 and 2016. Widescreen (16:9) slides have a size of 13.33 x 7.5 inches or 33.867 x 19.05 cm.
