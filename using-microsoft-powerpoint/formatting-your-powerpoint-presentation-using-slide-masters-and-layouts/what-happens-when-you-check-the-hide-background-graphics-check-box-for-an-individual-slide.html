FORMATTING YOUR POWERPOINT PRESENTATION USING SLIDE MASTERS AND LAYOUTS

What happens when you check the Hide background graphics check box for an individual slide?

(A) ** The graphics from the slide master are hidden on that slide.

(B) All the graphics are hidden on that slide.

(C) The selected graphics are hidden on that slide.

(D) Only the theme graphics are hidden on that slide.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -What happens when you check the Hide background graphics check box for an individual slide? The graphics from the theme and the slide master are hidden on that slide.

Concept note-2: -Click Format Background. In the Format Background pane, under Fill, click Solid Fill. Click the Color button and select white, or any other color you like. If you want to remove the background from all slides, click Apply to All.

Concept note-3: -Hide Master Background Objects on a Slide Select the Hide Background Graphics check box. To hide a background object on a single slide, display the slide in Normal view, click the Design tab, and then select the Hide Background Graphics check box.

Concept note-4: -Hide a slide In the left navigation pane, select the thumbnail image of the slide you want to hide. Right-click the slide, and then select Hide Slide. The slide now shows a slash through the slide number to indicate that it’s hidden.

Concept note-5: -If you don’t want a slide to be included in the final slide show, you can hide it from your audience. It won’t show up when you play the slide show, but you will still see it in the editing views so you can bring it back in the future. Select the slide you want to hide. Click the Slide Show tab.
