How many slides can be in one presentation.

(A) 1

(B) 5

(C) 7

(D) ** Multiple

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -As a curiosity more than a practical matter, PPT can theoretically (*theoretically* mind you) handle up to 2, 147, 483, 647 or so slides. At least, it can count that high. Other things would probably brick-wall you long before that.

Concept note-2: -When giving an hour long presentation, about 15 slides is ideal. Although having fewer slides might make you panic about possibly forgetting what you want to say, in reality fewer slides gives you more flexibility to narrate your idea journey in a dynamic way.

Concept note-3: -To save the venture capital community from death-by-PowerPoint, he evangelized the 10/20/30 rule for presentations which states that “a presentation should have ten slides, last no more than twenty minutes, and contain no font smaller than thirty points.”

Concept note-4: -“Keep it to one slide for every three minutes.” Even presentation pros like Guy Kawasaki will advocate for the 10/20/30 rule (10 slides, 20 minutes, 30 point font).
