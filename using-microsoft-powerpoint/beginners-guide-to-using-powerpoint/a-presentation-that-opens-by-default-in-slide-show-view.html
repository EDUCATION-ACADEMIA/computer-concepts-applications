USING MICROSOFT POWERPOINT

BEGINNERS GUIDE TO USING POWERPOINT

[SOURCES]
A presentation that opens by default in Slide Show view.

(A) PDF

(B) PowerPoint XPF Presentation

(C) ** PowerPoint Show

(D) PowerPoint Macro-Enabled Presentation

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -By default, PowerPoint opens in Normal view, displaying the thumbnails, notes and slide view. If you prefer, however, you can specify that PowerPoint open in a different view, such as Slide Sorter view, Slide Show view, Notes Page view, and variations on Normal view.

Concept note-2: -From the desktop, right-click your PowerPoint file, then select Show from the drop-down menu. Your file will open in Slide Show mode, where you can present it. To leave your presentation, just press Escape to return to the desktop.

Concept note-3: -Microsoft assumes that the first slide in your presentation will be for the title, so when you create a new presentation, a Title slide will be inserted by default.

Concept note-4: -Below are the three types of windows: Presented by a speaker (full screen): This is the default option. Slide Show view is full screen and you can click from slide to slide.

Concept note-5: -Slide Show view occupies the full computer screen, exactly the way your presentation will look on a big screen when your audience sees it.
