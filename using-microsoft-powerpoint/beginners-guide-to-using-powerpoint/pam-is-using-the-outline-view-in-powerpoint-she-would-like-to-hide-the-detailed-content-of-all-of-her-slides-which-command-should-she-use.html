BEGINNERS GUIDE TO USING POWERPOINT

[SOURCES]
Pam is using the outline view in PowerPoint. She would like to hide the detailed content of all of her slides. Which command should she use?

(A) Collapse

(B) ** Collapse All

(C) Demote

(D) Move Down

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Hide a slide In the left navigation pane, select the thumbnail image of the slide you want to hide. Right-click the slide, and then select Hide Slide. The slide now shows a slash through the slide number to indicate that it’s hidden.

Concept note-2: -Working in Outline view is particularly handy if you want to make global edits, get an overview of your presentation, change the sequence of bullets or slides, or apply formatting changes. When you’re creating your outline, think about how many slides you need.

Concept note-3: -If necessary, click the View tab in the Ribbon and select Normal in the Presentation Views group. In the Navigation pane on the left, right-click the slide that you want to hide. A drop-down menu appears. Click Hide Slide.

Concept note-4: -In a new PowerPoint presentation file, click the first slide. Or, in an existing presentation file, click the slide that you want the outline to appear after. On the Home tab, click the arrow next to New Slide, and then click Outline.
