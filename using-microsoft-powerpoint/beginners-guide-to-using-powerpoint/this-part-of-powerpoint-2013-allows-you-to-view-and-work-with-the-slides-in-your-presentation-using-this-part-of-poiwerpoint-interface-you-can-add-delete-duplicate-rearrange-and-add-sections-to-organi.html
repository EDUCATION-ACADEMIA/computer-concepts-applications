USING MICROSOFT POWERPOINT

BEGINNERS GUIDE TO USING POWERPOINT

[SOURCES]
This part of PowerPoint 2013 allows you to view and work with the slides in your presentation. Using this part of PoiwerPoint interface you can add, delete, duplicate, rearrange and add sections to organize and divide your slides.

(A) ** Slide Navigation Pane

(B) Slide Control Pane

(C) Slide Sorter Pane

(D) Slide Manager Pane

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The View tab allows you to switch between several different views for your presentation, including Outline View, Slide Sorter, and Slide Master. These views can help you prepare and organize your slide show.

Concept note-2: -The slide sorter lets you see your slides on the screen in a grid that makes it easy to reorganize them, or organize them into sections, just by dragging and dropping them where you want them.

Concept note-3: -PowerPoint also has a variety of different views that let you edit, arrange, or look at your slides in different ways. To change views, use the buttons on the View tab in the Presentation Views group, or View icons at the bottom right of the screen.

Concept note-4: -Notes pane is located below the slide pane and is used to type reference notes. The notes can be printed, then referenced when making the presentation. View area is located at the right hand bottom of the screen. It contains buttons that allow the ability to switch between PowerPoint views.
