Choosing the appropriate typeface can help determine the tone and message of your presentation.

(A) ** true

(B) false

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Generally, serif typefaces are easier to read for lengthy copy than sans. Serif fonts help the eye travel across a line, especially if lines are long. But it’s also important to consider your target audience. Sans is preferable for young children, or anyone just learning to read.

Concept note-2: -The chosen font or fonts should fit the mood and tone of the message and be easily legible. If you can master the skills of font selection and pairing, the value of your graphic design work will reach new heights.

Concept note-3: -Why is typography important? Typography is so much more than just choosing beautiful fonts: it’s a vital component of user interface design. Good typography will establish a strong visual hierarchy, provide a graphic balance to the website, and set the product’s overall tone.
