USING THE INSERT AND DESIGN MENUS IN POWERPOINT

[SOURCES]
Cropping may be helpful when a picture has a lot of ____ and you want to focus on only part of it.

(A) ** content

(B) options

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -To “crop” an image is to remove or adjust the outside edges of an image (typically a photo) to improve framing or composition, draw a viewer’s eye to the image subject, or change the size or aspect ratio. In other words, image cropping is the act of improving a photo or image by removing the unnecessary parts.

Concept note-2: -Crop the margins of a picture Click the picture, then click the Picture Format tab on the toolbar ribbon. On the Picture Format tab, select Crop. Black crop handles appear on the edges and corners of the picture. Crop the image by dragging any crop handle inward.

Concept note-3: -In your file, select the picture that you want to crop. On the Picture Format tab, click the arrow next to Crop. (If you don’t see the Picture Format tab, make sure that you’ve selected a picture (not a shape).) Point to Crop to Shape and then click the shape you want to crop to.

Concept note-4: -Cropping photos is a great tool to discard of unwanted areas in your image however it is important to keep in mind that when you crop an image this can have an effect on the quality of the image as cropping involves cutting away part of the original image resulting in some of the pixels being discarded.
