What are the empty boxes that appear on a new slide called that are used to reserve space for the information you want to insert

(A) Slide Master

(B) Slide Sorter

(C) ** Placeholders

(D) Slide

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Placeholders are boxes on a slide layout that you click to add content when you’re creating a presentation. In slide master view, you can add placeholders to a new slide layout, or change the look of an existing slide layout.

Concept note-2: -Placeholders – empty boxes that appear on a new slide that reserve a space for the type of information you want to insert.

Concept note-3: -Content placeholders are already formatted areas within in the slide layout where we can place different types of content such as, text, tables, charts, SmartArt, pictures, clipart or video. A Content placeholder is a placeholder you see on the typical Title and Content slide layout.

Concept note-4: -Add slides Select Home > New Slide. Select a layout.

Concept note-5: -Placeholders are the dotted-line containers on slide layouts that hold such content as titles, body text, tables, charts, SmartArt graphics, pictures, clip art, videos, and sounds.
