What are the best fonts and sizes for headings and body text?

(A) Times New Roman font, 15 for headings and 20 for body text.

(B) Any font will suffice. Use no less than 24 point throughout.

(C) ** Any clear San Serif font. 36-44 for headings 28-36 point for body text.

(D) Various different fonts and sizes, as long as it fits all the information.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Serif fonts are easily readable in smaller copy, while sans-serif fonts stand out in large, bold titles. Serif fonts read as “traditional” while sans-serif fonts tend to be read as “modern.” As seen above, serif fonts have extra embellishments while sans-serif fonts tend to be geometric and unadorned.

Concept note-2: -Serif fonts are ideal for body text. They improve the reading experience especially when reading long texts. But highly legible typefaces are also useful for longer titles. Sans serif fonts dominate on the web, however, in the print world serif typefaces are still top choice in terms of legibility.

Concept note-3: -Open Sans If you’re looking for a versatile font, Open Sans is your go-to. Open Sans is a clean sans serif typeface created by Steve Matteson in 2010. It’s one of the most popular sans serif fonts in use today thanks to its legibility on digital and print platforms alike.

Concept note-4: -Headings-Headings should be around 1.3 times larger than your body text to create a sufficient contrast. This would mean if you use 16px for body font size, then you would use  21px.
