Being Ethical means:

(A) ** Being a good person and doing the right thing

(B) Being a bad person and doing the bad thing

(C) Being very hungry

(D) Acting like a potato

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Ethics is based on well-founded standards of right and wrong that prescribe what humans ought to do, usually in terms of rights, obligations, benefits to society, fairness, or specific virtues.

Concept note-2: -Being ethical means you will do the right thing regardless of whether there are possible consequences-you treat other people well and behave morally for its own sake, not because you are afraid of the possible consequences. Simply put, people do the right thing because it is the right thing to do.

Concept note-3: -Ethical comes from the Greek ethos “moral character” and describes a person or behavior as right in the moral sense-truthful, fair, and honest. Sometimes the word is used for people who follow the moral standards of their profession.

Concept note-4: -Ethical relativism is the theory that holds that morality is relative to the norms of one’s culture. That is, whether an action is right or wrong depends on the moral norms of the society in which it is practiced. The same action may be morally right in one society but be morally wrong in another.
