What is the function of network policies in an organisation?

(A) Authenticates users.

(B) Forms an agreement between organisation and user regarding networkâ€™s usage.

(C) Defines the configuration of browsers.

(D) Establishes a plan to recover from security failure.

(E) ** All of the above

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Benefits of network policy Align the network with business needs. Provide consistent services across the entire infrastructure. Bring agility through greater automation. Make performance dependable and verifiable.

Concept note-2: -They include Acceptable Use, Disaster Recovery, Back-up, Archiving and Failover policies. People who need access to a network to do their job are usually asked to sign an agreement that they will only use it for legitimate reasons related to doing their job before they are allowed access.

Concept note-3: -If you want to control traffic flow at the IP address or port level (OSI layer 3 or 4), NetworkPolicies allow you to specify rules for traffic flow within your cluster, and also between Pods and the outside world. Your cluster must use a network plugin that supports NetworkPolicy enforcement.

Concept note-4: -A network security policy can limit security threats by defining which network assets must be protected and describing the practices and guidelines that will protect the security of these assets.
