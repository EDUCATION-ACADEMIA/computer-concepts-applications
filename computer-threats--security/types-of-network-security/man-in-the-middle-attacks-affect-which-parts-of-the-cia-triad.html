TYPES OF NETWORK SECURITY

[SOURCES]
Man-in-the-middle attacks affect which part(s) of the CIA triad?

(A) Confidentiality only

(B) Integrity only

(C) Availability only

(D) ** Confidentiality and Integrity

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Confidentiality is all about making sure that data is accessible only to its intended parties. Attacks on confidentiality can include: Cracking encrypted data. Man-in-the-middle attacks on plaintext data which is intended to be private (Why isn’t it encrypted then?)

Concept note-2: -In this context, confidentiality is a set of rules that limits access to information, integrity is the assurance that the information is trustworthy and accurate, and availability is a guarantee of reliable access to the information by authorized people.

Concept note-3: -Confidentiality means that data, objects and resources are protected from unauthorized viewing and other access. Integrity means that data is protected from unauthorized changes to ensure that it is reliable and correct. Availability means that authorized users have access to the systems and the resources they need.

Concept note-4: -Goals of CIA in Cyber Security Confidentiality: Preserving sensitive information confidential. Encryption services can save your data at rest or in transit and prevent unauthorized entry to shielded data. Integrity: is the consistency of data, networks, and systems.

Concept note-5: -The key purpose of cybersecurity is to ensure the Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA) of data and services. CIA or the CIA triad forms the foundation of any organization’s security infrastructure, indicating that once data or a system is attacked, these principles were violated.
