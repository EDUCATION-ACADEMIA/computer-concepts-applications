Law enforcement agencies can intercept data under what law? (7-9)

(A) Data Protection Act

(B) Regulation of Investigatory Powers Act (RIPA)

(C) Investigatory Powers Act

(D) ** Computer Misuse Act

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -In India, cyber crimes are covered by the Information Technology Act, 2000 and the Indian Penal Code, 1860. It is the Information Technology Act, 2000, which deals with issues related to cyber crimes and electronic commerce.

Concept note-2: -As a legally sanctioned official access to private communications, Lawful Interception (LI) is a security process in which a service provider or network operator collects and provides law enforcement officials with intercepted communications of private individuals or organizations.

Concept note-3: -The Cybercrime Prevention Act of 2012 also penalizes offenses against the privacy, confidentiality, integrity, and availability of computer data and systems, such as illegal access, unauthorized interference, system interference, data interference, misuse of devices, and cybersquatting.

Concept note-4: -The offences are: unauthorised access to computer material. unauthorised access with intent to commit or facilitate commission of further offences. unauthorised acts with intent to impair, or with recklessness as to impairing, operation of computer, etcetera.
