Who owns and controls the internet?

(A) Google

(B) Government

(C) Schools

(D) ** Nobody

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -No one person, company, organization or government runs the Internet. It is a globally distributed network comprising many voluntarily interconnected autonomous networks. It operates without a central governing body with each constituent network setting and enforcing its own policies.

Concept note-2: -The Internet is, in a way, more of a concept than a physical entity. No person has a patent or copyright over the internet. Instead, parts of the internet (data centers, cabling, satellites, routers, etc.) are owned by countless individuals, companies, and government agencies.

Concept note-3: -ICANN is responsible for technical operations of root and domain names infrastructure; it also acts as a transnational governance institution that makes global Internet public policies that impact copyright, privacy, and cybersecurity, which are sovereign interests of the nations.

Concept note-4: -India supports a multi-stakeholder approach in matters on Internet Governance. On matters relating to national security, Government will continue to have supreme right and control.
