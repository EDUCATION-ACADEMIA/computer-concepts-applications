Your boss is trying to create the new security policy for the organization in regard to mobile device security. He is leaning towards using a CYOD policy instead of a BYOD policy to increase security. Which of the following is a benefit of using a BYOD policy instead of a CYOD policy?

(A) ** Using a BYOD policy is less expensive than a CYOD policy

(B) Using a BYOD policy allows the company to have better control over the devices

(C) Using the BYOD policy will allow the company to own all the information on a given device

(D) Using a BYOD policy allows for easier management and patch management

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -BYOD (bring your own device) is a policy that allows employees in an organization to use their personally owned devices for work-related activities. Those activities include tasks such as accessing emails, connecting to the corporate network, and accessing corporate apps and data.

Concept note-2: -The acronyms themselves are easy: BYOD is Bring Your Own Device; CYOD is Choose Your Own Device; COPE is Company Owned/Personally Enabled; and COBO is Company Owned/Business Only.

Concept note-3: -Advantages: Lowest hardware and service costs, fastest deployment time, minimum wireless carrier management requirements, most convenient option for employees. Disadvantages: Higher configuration costs, difficult to enforce security (leading to enhanced legal implications), more complex support definitions.

Concept note-4: -BYOD is a fairly common device ownership and management model that almost every organization implements. In this model, employees are granted permission to use their personal mobile devices to connect to their organizational networks.
