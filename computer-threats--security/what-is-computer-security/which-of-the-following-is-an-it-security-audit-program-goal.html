Which of the following is an IT security audit program goal?

(A) Provide an objective and independent review of an organization’s policies, information systems and controls.

(B) Provide reasonable assurance that appropriate and effective IT controls are in place.

(C) Provide audit recommendations for both corrective actions and improvement to controls.

(D) ** All of the answers are correct.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Which of the following is an IT security audit program goal? Provide an objective and independent review of an organization’s policies, information systems and controls. Provide reasonable assurance that appropriate and effective IT controls are in place.

Concept note-2: -Reliability and integrity of information. Safeguarding of assets. Effective and efficient use of resources. Compliance with significant policies, procedures, laws and regulations.

Concept note-3: -The primary objectives of an IT audit include: Evaluate the systems and processes in place that secure company data. Determine risks to a company’s information assets, and help identify methods to minimize those risks.

Concept note-4: -Information Security Audit is a way for organizations to evaluate their security systems and identify flaws in them. The assessment helps in identifying vulnerabilities and discovering any potential entry points and security flaws that hackers may compromise to gain access into systems and networks.
