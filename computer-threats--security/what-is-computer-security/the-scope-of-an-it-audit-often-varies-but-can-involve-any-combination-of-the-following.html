WHAT IS COMPUTER SECURITY

[SOURCES]
The scope of an IT audit often varies, but can involve any combination of the following:

(A) organizational, compliance, application and social

(B) ** organizational, compliance, application and technical

(C) regional, compliance, application and technical

(D) organizational, compliance, systems and technical

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The scope of audit includes the assessment of internal controls in the auditable entities. Such an assessment may be undertaken either as an integral component of an audit or as a distinct audit assignment.

Concept note-2: -Audit Scope Meaning Audits are performed for several purposes: regular “checkups” of company records, to check for internal errors, for the purpose of finding fraud inside a company, for the purpose of finding fraud in another company, or even for the purpose of finding tax income and other offenses against IRS law.

Concept note-3: -IT audits is an examination of management controls within IT infrastructure. It not only examines physical security controls but also the business and financial controls that involve information technology systems.

Concept note-4: -The primary objectives of an IT audit include: Evaluate the systems and processes in place that secure company data. Determine risks to a company’s information assets, and help identify methods to minimize those risks.
