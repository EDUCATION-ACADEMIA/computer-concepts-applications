Any resemblance to actual persons, living or dead, or actual events is purely coincidental.

(A) ** Hard to guess + Easy to remember

(B) Includes Unicode characters + Easy to increment

(C) Hard to guess + Unicode

(D) Easy to remember + easy to increment

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -DON’T do the following: DON’T make obvious choices like your nickname, birthdate, spouse name, pet name, make/model of car, or favorite expression. DON’T share your password with anyone. DON’T use blank spaces in your password.

Concept note-2: -When it’s time to change your password, what’s the best way to choose a new one? A. Add a number or special character to the end of your old password. Pick something easy to remember, such as a football team or your birthday.

Concept note-3: -A strong must be at least 8 characters long. It should not contain personal information like real name, username, or company name. It must be very unique from your previously used passwords. It should not contain any word spelled completely.
