(D) ** A type of high-capacity DVD that is commonly used for high definition video.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -The Blu-ray Disc (BD), often known simply as Blu-ray, is a digital optical disc data storage format. It was invented and developed in 2005 and released on June 20, 2006 worldwide. It is designed to supersede the DVD format, and capable of storing several hours of high-definition video (HDTV 720p and 1080p).

Concept note-2: -Blu-ray, optical disc data-storage format that is most often used for playback of high-definition (HD) video. Blu-ray represents the third generation of compact disc (CD) technology, after audio CDs and digital video discs (DVDs).

Concept note-3: -HD DVD is the only high-def disc format that can decode 1080p25 while Blu-ray and HD DVD can both decode 1080p24 and 1080p30.

Concept note-4: -Blu-ray Disc media include these features: 50GB capacity-Each disc can hold more than 10 standard DVDs.

Concept note-5: -A standard DVD can store up to 4.7 GB of storage, while variants can store up to a maximum of 17.08 GB.
