. In which situation would it be okay to store your username/password on a computer so you can log in faster?

(A) At the Apple Store

(B) At your local library

(C) ** In the computer lab where you sit everyday

(D) At your best friend’s house

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Passwords provide the first line of defense against unauthorized access to your computer and personal information. The stronger your password, the more protected your computer will be from hackers and malicious software. You should maintain strong passwords for all accounts on your computer.

Concept note-2: -Go to the website for which you want to save your data. Fill in your login information and log in to the website. While the page is loading, Chrome will provide a prompt to save your password data. Click “Save Password."
