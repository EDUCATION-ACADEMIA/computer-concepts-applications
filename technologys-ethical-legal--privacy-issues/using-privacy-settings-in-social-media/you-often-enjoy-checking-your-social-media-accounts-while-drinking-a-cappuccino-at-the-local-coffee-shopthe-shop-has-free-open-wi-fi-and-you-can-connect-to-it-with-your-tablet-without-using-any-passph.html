USING PRIVACY SETTINGS IN SOCIAL MEDIA

[SOURCES]
You often enjoy checking your social media accounts while drinking a cappuccino at the local coffee shop.The shop has free open Wi-Fi and you can connect to it with your tablet without using any passphrase. However, one of your friends told you that there might be dangers with such open networks.What could these dangers be?

(A) There might be extra hidden charges that I am not aware of.

(B) Other people connected to the open network can see what you are doing.

(C) ** Personal data can be stolen from my communications on the open Wi-Fi.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Report harassment or inappropriate content. If someone is making you feel uncomfortable online, you can report the interaction to the host site, often anonymously. You can use the “report” button near the chat window, flag a post as inappropriate, or submit a screenshot of the interaction directly to the host site.

Concept note-2: -Browser: A software application used to locate and display Web pages. The two most popular browsers are Microsoft Internet Explorer and Netscape Navigator.

Concept note-3: -The more time spent on social media can lead to cyberbullying, social anxiety, depression, and exposure to content that is not age appropriate.

Concept note-4: -Enforcing communication via secure channels. Performing strong identity verification to ensure devices are not compromised. Limiting the use of third-party software and browsing to unsafe websites. Encrypting data on the device to protect against device compromise and theft.
