an open source version of Unix developed by Lin Torvalds Linux was originally developed by Lin Torvalds, who wanted a free Unix-like operating system that ran on standard PC hardware.

(A) ** Linux

(B) Command Line Entry

(C) Gui (Graphical User Interface)

(D) Encryption

(E) Compression

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Linus Torvalds had wanted to call his invention “Freax", a portmanteau of “free", “freak", and “x” (as an allusion to Unix).

Concept note-2: -Linux was created in 1991 by Linus Torvalds, a then-student at the University of Helsinki. Torvalds built Linux as a free and open source alternative to Minix, another Unix clone that was predominantly used in academic settings.

Concept note-3: -Linux began in 1991 as a personal project by Finnish student Linus Torvalds: to create a new free operating system kernel. The resulting Linux kernel has been marked by constant growth throughout its history.

Concept note-4: -Linux is a Unix-like, open source and community-developed operating system (OS) for computers, servers, mainframes, mobile devices and embedded devices.

Concept note-5: -The correct answer is Linux. Key Points Linux: One of the most successful open-source products is the Linux operating system, an open-source Unix-like operating system, and its derivative Android, an operating system for mobile devices.
