[SOURCES]
a unit of electric current equal to a flow of one coulomb per second

(A) Electricity

(B) Python

(C) Input

(D) ** Ampere

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -When 1 coulomb of charge flows through any cross-section of a conductor in 1 second, the electric current through it is said to be 1 ampere.

Concept note-2: -The ampere (symbol: A) is the SI base unit of electric current equal to one coulomb per second.

Concept note-3: -In terms of SI base units, 1 C is the equivalent of 1 ampere-second, which can be expressed as 1 C = 1 A ⋅ 1 s (or C = A ⋅ s). This can also be thought of as an ampere being equal to 1 coulomb divided by 1 s, as in 1 A = 1 C / 1 s (or A = C / s).

Concept note-4: -1 ampere of current is equivalent to 1 coulomb per second i.e when 1 coulomb of charge flows through a conductor in 1 second of time then 1 ampere of current is said to have flown through the conductor.

Concept note-5: -The ampere (/ˈæmpɛər/, US: /ˈæmpɪər/; symbol: A), often shortened to amp, is the unit of electric current in the International System of Units (SI). One ampere is equal to 1 coulomb or 6.241509074×1018 electrons worth of charge moving past a point in a second.
