RASPBERRY PI PROJECT IDEAS

[SOURCES]
Through ____ port, Raspberry Pi is connected to the TV Screen.

(A) ** HDMI

(B) RCA

(C) SPI

(D) CSI

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Connect your HDMI cable from your Raspberry Pi to your monitor or TV. If you intend to connect your Raspberry Pi to the internet, plug an Ethernet cable into the Ethernet port, or connect a WiFi dongle to one of the USB ports (unless you have a Raspberry Pi 3 which has a WiFi chip already on the board).

Concept note-2: -HDMI. Your Raspberry Pi has an HDMI output port that is compatible with the HDMI port of most modern TVs and computer monitors. Many computer monitors may also have DVI or VGA ports. Raspberry Pi 4 has two micro HDMI ports, allowing you to connect two separate monitors.

Concept note-3: -Simply connect the HDMI cable to your Raspberry Pi on one end and PC monitor to another end. After the connection has been established, you will see the booting options with the usual graphical interface. If your PC monitor doesn’t support HDMI, you can use the alternate option of HDMI to VGA adapter.

Concept note-4: -All Raspberry Pis Have HDMI Consequently, anyone can easily connect the device to their living room television or any other modern desktop monitor. Note that on the Raspberry Pi Zero, you will need a mini HDMI adapter. This isn’t the only version that requires an adapter to use HDMI, however.
