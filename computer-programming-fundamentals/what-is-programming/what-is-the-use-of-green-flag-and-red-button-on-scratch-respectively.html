What is the use of green flag and red button on Scratch respectively?

(A) To end and start a program.

(B) To change background of a program.

(C) ** To start and end a program.

(D) To pause and resume a program.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Stage – An area where you can see the output of the project and how it’s running by clicking on the green flag above it. You can stop running your project by clicking on the red button.

Concept note-2: -The “green flag” button is used to run a script in Scratch programming. The button is located just above the stage area, next to the “red button”. The “red button” is used to stop the script.

Concept note-3: -Whenever you click the green flag, your script will start. To stop, click the stop button.

Concept note-4: -Flags: Flags are used to execute the scripts. The green flag starts the execution whereas the red flag stops the execution.

Concept note-5: -A green flag is part of a set of racing flags and indicates the beginning or resumption of an auto race. The actual flags flown in parks and gardens that have received the Green Flag Award.
