(A) Machine

(B) ** Assembly

(C) Low level

(D) High level

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Assembly or assembler language was the second generation of computer language. By the late 1950s, this language had become popular. Assembly language consists of letters of the alphabet. This makes programming much easier than trying to program a series of zeros and ones.

Concept note-2: -Second Generation: By 1950, computers were being programmed in assembly language. As we saw earlier, one line in Assembly Language is translated into one machine instruction by a program called an assembler. At this time, computers were so expensive and slow that this was the only feasible method of programming.
