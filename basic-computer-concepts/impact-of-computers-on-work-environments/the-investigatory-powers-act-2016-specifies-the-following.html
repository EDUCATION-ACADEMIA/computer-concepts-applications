BASIC COMPUTER CONCEPTS

IMPACT OF COMPUTERS ON WORK ENVIRONMENTS

[SOURCES]
The Investigatory Powers Act 2016 specifies the following:

(A) ** Phone companies and internet service providers are required to keep copies of users’ emails and browsing histories for 12 months.

(B) The police have no right to look at private browsing histories.

(C) Phone companies and internet service providers are required to keep copies of users’ emails and browsing histories forever

(D) The security services have no right to look at phone records.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -It makes provision for the retention of internet connection records for law enforcement to identify the communications service to which a device has connected. This will restore capabilities that have been lost as a result of changes in the way people communicate.

Concept note-2: -The Investigatory Powers Act 2016 provides that communications service providers may be required by the Secretary of State to retain communications data, for up to 12 months, where it is considered necessary and proportionate to do so and where that decision has been approved by a Judicial Commissioner.

Concept note-3: -(3)The power to make regulations under subsection (1) includes power to make such modifications in any enactment (including this Act) as the Secretary of State considers appropriate in consequence of a person becoming, or ceasing to be, a relevant public authority because of regulations under that subsection.
