Like Supercomputer, mainframes are very large with a large memory capacity. ____

(A) ** True

(B) False

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -A mainframe computer is large but not as large as a supercomputer and has more processing power than some other classes of computers, such as minicomputers, servers, workstations, and personal computers.

Concept note-2: -What is a Mainframe Computer? These types of computers are also pretty advanced, but they are comparatively smaller in size than the supercomputers.

Concept note-3: -Rapid retrieval of stored data and instructions is required to support the extremely high computational speed of CPUs. Therefore, most supercomputers have a very large storage capacity, as well as a very fast input/output capability.

Concept note-4: -During their largest period, in terms of physical size, a typical mainframe occupied 2, 000 to 10, 000 square feet (600 to 3000 square meters).
