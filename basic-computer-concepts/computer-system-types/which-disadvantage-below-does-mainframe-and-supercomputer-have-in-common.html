Which disadvantage below does Mainframe and Supercomputer have in common?

(A) They are both inexpensive

(B) They require constantly charging the batteries

(C) ** They both require a large amount of space

(D) They cost roughly the same amount and are expensive alike.

EXPLANATIONS BELOW

Concept note-1: -Which disadvantage below does Mainframe and Supercomputer have in common? They cost roughly the same amount and are expensive alike.

Concept note-2: -Both types of computers perform different tasks. Supercomputers basically focus on faster computing for the mathematical operations that might be complex in nature. The mainframe computer, on the other hand, acts like a server. It offers multiprogramming, many I/O devices, and provides support for large DB (databases).

Concept note-3: -Main Disadvantages: One of the prominent drawbacks of mainframes is their cost. Hardware and software for mainframes are clearly expensive. Secondly, mainframe hardware occupies more space than other computers. Finally, one needs high-end skills to work with mainframe computers.

Concept note-4: -1. Supercomputers are used for large and complex mathematical computations. While Mainframe computers are used as a storage for large databases and serve as a maximum number of users simultaneously.

Concept note-5: -The main difference between a mainframe and a super computer of Supercomputers are focused to execute few programs as fast as possible while mainframe uses its power to execute as many programs concurrently.
